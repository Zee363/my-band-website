The Soil

The Soil is a South African a cappella group from Soweto founded in 2003, consisting of the lead singer Ntsika Ngxanga, beatboxer Luphindo, and vocalist Theo Matshoba. The band eponymous debut LP The Soil, which became certified Platinum by the Recording Industry of South Africa.

Debut album - "The Soil"

The inspiration behind the debut album was the melting pot of the group's youthful spirits at the time. The angst experienced in their young relationships; their relationship with God; their perspective on how they viewed the world and what they thought it should look like in their day-to-day occurances played a huge role of how the album came to be. The album was released just after the Fifa World Cup that was hosted in South Africa. It rekindled the spirit of unity that South Africa had during that time, instilling togetherness that at the end of the day, we are all one big family whether we know each other or not.
